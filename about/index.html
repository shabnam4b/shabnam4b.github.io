About



Hello! I am Shabnam! I am a software engineer. I have been working on problems related to system security most of my time at work and graduate school. Currently, I work on autonomous driving technology at Argo AI. Some of my past projects are:

  • Design and implementation of a dynamic binary analysis tool (SEMU) under supervison of Professor Chirstoph Csallner.

  • Implementation of user-mode code injection detection techniques for Microsoft Windows Defender product (more info).

  • Integration of hardware-based system integrity approaches in Windows endpoint protection agent in order to perform runtime attestation of Windows kernel (more info, the blog post published after I left Microsoft).

  • Development of Android Verified Boot (VB1) for the first UEFI-based version of Qualcomm’s bootloader.

Although I still love software engineering and system security, I found myself spending most of my free times reading books and research papers or watching documentaries about Brain. I’m passionate about Neuroscience and my plan is to share my notes about my learnings in this website. I may also throw my notes about system security :). Hopefully, they will be useful to some science and tech enthusiasts.

Note: I am not a neuroscientist (yet), I cannot guarantee the contents of my posts are fully accurate and up-to-date.
